We are the MHSS Puthencavu unit of the National Service Scheme (NSS), under the Ministry of Youth Affairs & Sports, Government of India. Our unit was established in 1970, our four units, guided by dedicated coordinators, engage over 100 student volunteers in meaningful community service in and around the campus, promoting social responsibility.

WHAT DO WE DO?

At MHSS, the NSS unit actively involves students in various activities that help develop their skills and values. We conduct teaching programs for underprivileged children, organize workshops, awareness camps, and invite experts for interactive sessions. Our students have taken part in cleanliness drives, blood donation awareness, and even hosted a mini-marathon. Initiatives like Sneharagam reflect our commitment to social responsibility. Through these efforts, NSS at MHSS plays a key role in shaping responsible, community-focused individuals.

WORKS

Campus

NSS volunteers actively contribute to maintaining a clean, green, and inclusive campus environment.

Community

We engage in impactful outreach programs, focusing on education, health, and sustainability in nearby villages.

Orientation

New volunteers undergo orientation sessions to understand the values, objectives, and responsibilities of NSS service.

Haritha Bhoomi

As part of fostering agricultural knowledge among students, vegetables were planted in the homes of participating village residents. This hands-on initiative aimed to promote sustainable farming practices and encourage students to develop practical skills in agriculture. By engaging in home gardening, students gained firsthand experience in cultivating vegetables, learning about soil preparation, plant care, and the importance of growing their own food. The program not only enhanced their understanding of agriculture but also promoted healthy eating habits and environmental awareness within the community.

Teachers Day

The NSS volunteers organized a heartfelt celebration for Teachers' Day, honoring the dedicated educators who shape young minds and inspire future generations. The celebration also featured interactive games and fun activities that brought teachers and students closer together, creating memorable moments of joy and laughter.

Mob Flash

A thought-provoking musical sculpture illustrating the impact of substance abuse was presented to children at Puthancavu Junction. This creative initiative demonstrated how art can serve as an effective tool for raising awareness about substance abuse among children, making the important message memorable and impactful through interactive and visual storytelling.
